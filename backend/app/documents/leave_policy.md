# Corporate Leave Policy & Procedures Framework

**Document Identifier:** HR-LVP-2026-V1  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026  
**Jurisdiction:** Global Operations  

---

## 1. Executive Summary & Core Eligibility
The organization values sustainable work-life integration and health management. This policy establishes a transparent, equitable framework for employee time-off, covering all regular full-time and part-time staff. Contractors are governed explicitly by their independent project service contracts.

---

## 2. Comprehensive Leave Categories

### 2.1 Annual Paid Time Off (PTO)
Full-time personnel accumulate PTO hours based on continuous organizational tenure. This balance covers personal relaxation, family vacations, and general leisure.

| Completed Tenure | Annual PTO Accrual Baseline | Monthly Earned Rate | Maximum Roll-over Limit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **0 – 2 Years** | 15 Working Days | 1.25 Days | 5 Days Max |
| **3 – 5 Years** | 20 Working Days | 1.66 Days | 7 Days Max |
| **6+ Years** | 25 Working Days | 2.08 Days | 10 Days Max |

### 2.2 Statutory Sick & Wellness Leave
Employees receive **10 paid sick days per year**, allocated on January 1st. This bucket supports recovery from illness, medical appointments, or caring for sick family members. A formal medical certificate from a licensed doctor is required for absences exceeding three (3) consecutive working days.

### 2.3 Comprehensive Parental Leave
To support expanding families, we provide **16 weeks of fully paid parental leave** to biological, adoptive, or foster parents. This benefit is accessible following twelve (12) consecutive months of full-time employment.

### 2.4 Bereavement & Compassionate Leave
Employees are eligible for up to **5 consecutive paid days** of bereavement leave for the loss of an immediate family member (spouse, child, parent, sibling). For extended relatives, 3 working days are provisioned.

### 2.5 Extended Sabbatical Framework
Personnel completing five (5) consecutive years of service can request a **4-week unpaid or partially subsidized sabbatical** to pursue academic goals, research, or personal enrichment, with guaranteed role preservation upon return.

---

## 3. Operational Request & Approval Workflows
1. **Submission Window:** Standard PTO requests must be submitted through the internal HR Portal at least **two (2) weeks before** the intended start date.
2. **Manager Review:** Direct managers evaluate requests based on project timelines and team coverage, returning an approval or modification decision within three (3) business days.
3. **Blackout Windows:** Peak operational periods (e.g., Q4 fiscal closeout cycles) may have strict resource restrictions. Plan time-off outside these windows whenever possible.

---

## 4. Financial Calculations & Separation Accruals
- **Accrual Baseline:** PTO is earned dynamically each pay period. Unused hours roll over automatically up to the caps specified in Section 2.1.
- **Excess Leave:** PTO balances exceeding the rollover cap at midnight on December 31st expire automatically to encourage regular rest.
- **Separation Payouts:** Upon departure, employees receive a full cash payout for all remaining, legally accrued PTO days, based on their standard base salary rate at the time of separation.

---

## 5. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

#### Q: Can I borrow against future unearned PTO hours if I exhaust my current balance?
**A:** With written approval from both your Department Head and HR Business Partner, you can run a negative balance of up to five (5) working days for urgent personal matters.

#### Q: How does taking extended parental leave affect my annual promotion cycle?
**A:** Taking approved statutory or company leave has zero impact on performance tracks or promotion eligibility. Your performance is evaluated relative to active working time.
